The renewal cycle

Six steps, one workspace

01

Onboard the client

A guided five-part profile — company details, internal team and client contacts, locations, goals and constraints, and source documents — with progress tracked on the client overview.

02

Open the plan year

Proposes next year's label and defaults the effective date to January 1.

03

Import the census

Carrier workbooks up to 20 MB, parsed in memory. Each upload atomically replaces the last, and SSNs are discarded before anything is stored.

04

Enter policy & rates

Every medical, dental, vision, life, and disability plan or class, with two-, three-, or four-tier rates reconciled to the cent.

05

Benchmark the market

Compare calculated rates and provisions against the national Mercer baseline and a matched peer cohort — automatically, or by override.

06

Curate & generate

Enable the analyses that matter, choose alternate views, and export a branded PowerPoint with data-derived takeaways and recommendations.

What's inside

Built for the full renewal, not just the deck

Onboarding

Guided client profiles

Five progressive sections capture company profile, team and client contacts, organization and locations, goals and constraints, and private source documents — with visible completion progress.

Rates

A real policy & rate engine

Unlimited plans per benefit, two-, three-, or four-tier rate structures, explicit rate periods, enrollment overrides, and a readiness panel that surfaces missing rates before they reach a deck.

Benchmarking

Mercer market benchmarking

Every medical plan year is checked against a versioned, national Mercer dataset and a recommended industry, size, or geographic peer cohort — shown only where a real comparison exists.

Decks

Insight-led PowerPoint, not a raw export

A branded cover, section dividers, key-takeaway callouts on every applicable slide, and a closing recommendations slide generated only from measurable conditions in the data.

Data quality

Governance built in

A data quality appendix, participation and waiver tracking, and admin-managed user accounts keep every deck traceable back to the underlying census and rates.

Portability

Client records you can move

Export a client's complete profile, contacts, rates, and settings as a single .benefithq file, and import it back into any Benefit HQ workspace.

Security & access

Employee data handled deliberately

Benefit HQ is built for the sensitive data a renewal actually involves — names, dates of birth, salaries, and elections — not just chart-friendly numbers.

  • Census workbooks are parsed in memory; SSNs are used only to join records and are discarded before anything is persisted.
  • Generated decks, intake documents, and client logos are private files served only through authenticated requests.
  • Access isn't self-serve. Accounts are created by your firm's administrator from Settings.
  • Each firm runs in its own dedicated, isolated workspace — data never crosses between firms.
  • Archiving and permanent deletion are administrator-only, with deletion requiring the exact client name to confirm.
